Boo Banner

Supplies

  • 3/4 yard of black cotton fabric
  • 3/4 yard of single sided fusible ultra-firm stabilizer (I used Peltex 71F)
  • Fabric marking pencil/chalk
  • 23 inch long dowel (may need to cut down the length of your dowel if you purchased a longer one)
  • 26 inches to 28 inches strand of yarn
  • Scissors
  • Iron and ironing board
  • Boo letter template
  • Sewing machine

Instructions

  1. Fold your black fabric in half and cut out your banner. See dimensions pictured above.
  2. Cut your stabilizer smaller than the black fabric banner size. I did mine about 3 inches less than the length and about 1 inch less on the sides. Fuse the stabilizer and fabric together. Note: Place the stabilizer about 2 inch down from the top of the banner, to allow space to fold the top of the fabric down 1 inch to create the casing for the dowel.
  3. Cut out the Boo template (or your own block writing), center it onto the front of the fabric banner and trace with a fabric pencil or chalk.
  4. Cut out the letters.
  5. Fold the top of the banner down 1 inch, pin in place, and sew down the entire length about a 3/4 inch from the folded edge. This creates the dowel casing. Note: If you dont have access to a sewing machine, use fabric glue to hold the casing in place (or theres also good old fashion hand sewing!)
  6. Insert your dowel into the casing and attach your yarn to each end of the dowel.
